Tiger Woods: On November 27, 2009, after The National Enquirer had reported that the golfer had cheated on his wife, Elin Nordegren, with nightclub manager Rachel Uchitel, Woods hit a tree with his SUV outside his Florida home. With rumors flying that Nordegren had attacked him with a golf club, Woods called it a "private matter." But more than a dozen women came forward claiming to have had flings with Woods and some of the gals shared texts and voice mails that proved he was one of the worst celebrity cheaters ever. Woods finally admitted infidelity and apologized, but lost numerous endorsement deals and had to take a break from golf tournaments. Woods’ marriage to Nordegren ended in divorce in August 2010 but he got his career back and has recently been dating skier Lindsey Vonn.
G
John Travolta: Fans were shocked last year when a masseur filed a $2 million lawsuit against the Pulp Fiction star. The unnamed man accused Travolta of sexual assault and battery, saying the actor came on to him several times during a massage session at the Beverly Hills Hotel. A second anonymous masseur then joined the lawsuit, but the men later dropped their legal fight and the case was dismissed. It wasn’t the first time Travolta had been in a scandal involving alleged gay sex. In 1990, adult film producer Paul Baresi claimed in The National Enquirer that he’d had a two-year affair with the actor. Several months later, though, Baresi retracted his story.

Liberace: The flamboyant pianist met Scott Thorson, 17, in 1976 and hired him as his companion, which resulted in a five-year romantic relationship. In 1982, however, Liberace was tainted with scandal after Thorson filed a $113 million palimony lawsuit against him. The two settled out of court in 1986 and Thorson said he reconciled with Liberace shortly before the entertainer's death in 1987. Thorson’s later memoir about the relationship, Behind the Candelabra: My Life with Liberace, was adapted into the HBO movie that aired in May with Matt Damon as Thorson and Michael Douglas playing Liberace.
G
Frank Gifford/Kathie Lee Gifford: The former football great and his TV hostess wife seemed like the perfect couple—until The Globe revealed in 1997 that blonde flight attendant Suzen Johnson had a sexual fling with him at New York's Regency Hotel. At first the Giffords called Johnson’s story "total fabrication" but after the tabloid published video frames it had taped of the affair, the couple released a statement: “This experience has been….painful for us….we will get through this together.” Surprisingly, Frank and Kathie Lee did put it behind them and have now been married 26 years.
G
Rebecca Gayheart/Eric Dane/Kari Ann Peniche: The Grey’s Anatomy hunk was known as McDreamy—but fans saw his McSteamy side in 2009 after a nude tape featuring him, wife Rebecca Gayheart and former Miss Teen USA Kari Ann Peniche was aired on the Gawker web site. In the video, all three lay naked in bed, cavorted in a Jacuzzi, and discussed their porn names—but no sex acts were shown. Dane and Gayheart weathered the bizarre incident and went on to have two daughters together.
G
Hulk Hogan: A sex tape featuring the wrestling star and Heather Clem, the estranged wife of his friend, Todd Alan Clem (AKA radio DJ Bubba the Love Sponge), leaked out last year. Hogan later told Howard Stern that the sexcapade took place six years before while he was still married to his first wife, Linda. He claimed he didn’t know the sex was being taped and added he made a “bad choice” by cheating. In October 2012, Hogan sued Bubba for invasion of privacy, but the suit was soon settled out of court with the DJ apologizing to the WWE star. Hogan had married second wife, Jennifer McDaniel, in 2010.
G
Fatty Arbuckle: The rotund star of silent movie comedies was accused of killing actress Virginia Rappe, 30, on September 9, 1921 during a violent sex attack, allegedly rupturing her bladder. The funnyman went through three trials over the death that happened during his Labor Day weekend party but wasn't convicted. His exoneration came too late, however, as the scandal had wrecked already his career; Arbuckle died a broken man at 46 in 1933. The cause of Virginia Rappe’s death remains a Hollywood mystery.
G
Charlie Chaplin: The famed movie comedian got into a heap of legal trouble when actress Joan Barry announced she was pregnant with Chaplin’s child in 1943 and blabbed to the media about their affair. In 1944, Chaplin was accused with allegedly violating the Mann Act, which prohibited the transportation of women across state lines for sexual purposes. Chaplin was found not guilty, but he then faced a paternity trial regarding Barry’s baby, was declared the father, and was ordered to pay child support. Two weeks after the paternity suit had been filed, the Little Tramp had married his fourth wife, Oona O’Neill, 18. Chaplin finally settled down, staying with O’Neill until he died in 1977. They had eight children.
G
Pam Anderson and Tommy Lee: The former Baywatch babe and Motley Crue drummer’s home video of their honeymoon sex caused a stir after it was stolen from their home in 1995 and leaked online. In 1998, Pam sued the distribution company and the couple reportedly won $1.5 million in damages—with the company allowed to make money off the very popular tape!
Mackenzie Phillips: The former One Day at a Time sitcom star made a shocking revelation in her 2009 book, High on Arrival, saying she had sex with her own father, Mamas and Papas musician John Phillips, while under the influence of drugs. She controversially told Oprah Winfrey the incest, which allegedly happened for ten years “became a consensual relationship.” Mackenzie said she stopped having sex with Phillips (who died in 2001) when she became pregnant and, fearing her dad might be the baby’s father, had an abortion. Some of Phillips’ family members, however, denied Mackenzie’s incendiary claims.
Michael Jackson: After years of raising eyebrows by hanging out with young boys, the King of Pop was accused of child molestation in 2005. Although he was acquitted of sexually abusing Gavin Arvizo in the sensational trial, since Jackson died in 2009, more charges have surfaced. Dancer Wade Robson is suing the late singer’s estate, alleging Jackson molested him when he was little. And as RadarOnline had also recently reported, Britain’s PEOPLE magazine claimed that Jackson paid $35 million to silence 24 boys he abused over the years.
Kim Kardashian: Just months before Kim’s family show, Keeping Up With The Kardashians, debuted on E! in 2007, the beauty was caught in a sex tape scandal. A naughty video she’d made with her boyfriend Ray J in 2003 was leaked for the world to see. Ultimately, Kim dropped a planned lawsuit against Vivid Entertainment, which sought to sell the tape, and settled with the company for $5 million. The deal made people wonder if Kim might have leaked the tape herself for publicity!
LeAnn Rimes/Eddie Cibrian/Brandi Glanville: The country cutie and TV actor were both married to other people when they began filming the Lifetime movie Northern Lights together in 2009. But they soon started a torrid affair that resulted in tabloid headlines and the breakup of both star’s marriages—LeAnn’s to dancer Dean Sheremet and Eddie’s to future Real Housewives of Beverly Hills star Brandi Glanville. LeAnn and Eddie wound up getting married in 2011 after shedding their spouses but Brandi has never let them—or the public!—forget how their relationship started with a cheating scandal.
Pee-wee Herman: The Pee-wee’s Playhouse star’s arrest for allegedly masturbating at a Florida porn theater in 1991 shocked America. According to an AP report, police claimed Pee-wee, whose real name is Paul Reubens, offered to do a children’s benefit for the sheriff’s department to “take care of” the incident. Instead, he had to face the music and pleaded no contest to a misdemeanor charge of indecent exposure, while CBS stopped airing his children’s show. But it wasn’t Reubens’ last brush with the law--in 2004, he pleaded guilty to possessing obscene images of minors. However, Reubens also has insisted he was merely a collector of artistic erotica.
G
Roman Polanski: The famed director, whose pregnant wife Sharon Tate had been murdered by the Manson Family, was arrested himself in Los Angeles in 1977 for the sexual assault of Samantha Geimer, 13. During a photo shoot for French Vogue, Polanski, then 43, reportedly gave Geimer a Quaalude and champagne, and then raped her. He fled the U.S. for France before final sentencing. In 2009, the fugitive was arrested at the request of U.S. authorities while he was in Switzerland but the Swiss soon released Polanski, declaring him a free man. His victim Geimer has said she no longer wants Polanski prosecuted.
G
Charlie Sheen: Before the actor starred on Two and a Half Men and became a regular headline grabber, Sheen was notorious for hiring hookers from famed Hollywood madam Heidi Fleiss! After Fleiss was busted for running a prostitution ring in the early 1990s, Sheen had to testify at her trial for tax evasion and admitted to being a client, saying he spent more than $50,000 on Fleiss’ prostitutes in a two-year span.
David Letterman: In 2009, six months after marrying longtime girlfriend Regina Lasko, the Late Show host became embroiled in a sex scandal. He admitted on the air, “I have had sex with women who work for me on this show” and had faced an extortion bid over the liaisons. Earlier this year, Letterman admitted to Oprah Winfrey he’s still dealing with the fallout from the cheating on wife Regina, the mother of his son: “I’m still trying to fix it.”
Rob Lowe: Lowe’s career was damaged in 1989 after a tape was leaked showing him having sex with two girls, one of whom just 16. The Parks and Recreation star insists to this day he didn’t know she was underage. Another part of the tape, which showed Lowe and a male pal having sex with a model, was later sold as one of the first celebrity sex tapes. Lowe bounced back by making fun of himself on Saturday Night Live, achieving sobriety, getting married and having a family.
G
Woody Allen: The director began an illicit affair with Soon-Yi Previn, the adopted daughter of his longtime girlfriend Mia Farrow, in 1992. Farrow discovered the relationship after finding raunchy nude photos Allen had taken of Soon-Yi. After the press and public lambasted Allen, he defended himself for having sex with Soon-Yi, shrugging, "The heart wants what it wants." He and Soon-Yi are still going strong after marrying in 1997 and adopting two daughters together.
G
Kristen Stewart: Last year, the star made tabloid headlines for cheating on her boyfriend and Twilight co-star Robert Pattinson with her married Snow White and the Huntsman director Rupert Sanders. After the romance was exposed, Stewart publicly apologized to Pattinson and they later resumed their relationship. But recently, the two split and Pattinson is dating other women.
G
Brad Pitt/Angelina Jolie/Jennifer Aniston: The movie hunk was still married to Friends cutie Jennifer Aniston when sparks flew on the set with his sexy Mr. & Mrs. Smith co-star Angelina. The public couldn’t get enough news about the love triangle and Jen filed for divorce from Brad in 2005. Angelina seemed to confirm what everyone thought all along when she cooed in a 2008 interview that she was looking forward to having their brood of kids see Mr. & Mrs. Smith one day because “not a lot of people get to see a movie where their parents fell in love.”
G
Sandra Bullock and Jesse James: Five years after The Blind Side star wed motorcycle customizer Jesse James in 2005, several women claimed to have had affairs with him during the marriage. In the biggest bombshell, tattooed erotic dancer Michelle McGee said she had sex with James on the couch in his office behind Bullock’s back. James confessed, issuing a public apology to Bullock and asking her to forgive him. But in April 2010, the actress filed for divorce and she adopted a baby boy, Louis, as a single mom.
Hugh Grant: In 1995, Grant was arrested near Sunset Boulevard in Los Angeles for misdemeanor lewd conduct in a public place with Hollywood prostitute Divine Brown. The actor, who was in a relationship with Liz Hurley at the time, pleaded no contest and paid a fine. Just days after his arrest, Grant went on The Tonight Show and told Jay Leno, “I did a bad thing.” The gutsy move saved Grant’s career and the public forgave him.
G
Liz Taylor/Eddie Fisher/Debbie Reynolds: It was the biggest scandal of the 1950s and foreshadowed the Brangelina/Jennifer Aniston love triangle. While comforting Taylor over the death of her husband Mike Todd, Fisher—who was married to America’s sweetheart, Debbie Reynolds at the time--fell for the violet-eyed temptress himself. He divorced Reynolds and married Taylor in 1959. But the joke was on Fisher as Taylor later divorced him for her Cleopatra co-star, Richard Burton, whom she married in 1964.
G
Errol Flynn: In 1942, two underage girls accused the swashbuckling Captain Blood star of statutory rape. Although the sensational trial could have wrecked his career, Flynn was found not guilty when the jury didn’t believe the girls’ testimony. However, Flynn, who admitted liking his women young, saw his movie popularity skyrocket after the scandal, leading to the phrase “in like Flynn.”
G
Jude Law: In 2005, following reports in British tabloids, actor Jude Law admitted that he'd cheated on his fiancee Sienna Miller with his children's nanny. Law made a public apology, saying, "I am deeply ashamed and upset that I've hurt Sienna." Meanwhile, the nanny, Daisy Wright, did a tell-all interview in which she declared, “He is a great lover.” Law and Miller split but she got her own bad press three years later when she was photographed topless and kissing Brothers and Sisters star Balthazar Getty, a married man with four children.
G
Arnold Schwarzenegger: The movie star and California Governor’s good image was terminated after news broke that he’d not only cheated on his wife Maria Shriver with their housekeeper—but had secretly fathered a son with the woman! In 2011, Schwarzenegger admitted the relationship with Mildred Baena, saying “it was the stupidest thing” he’d ever done. It ended his 25-year marriage to the Kennedy family daughter and TV newswoman.
G
George Michael: In 1998, Michael was arrested by a male undercover police officer for engaging in a lewd act in a public bathroom in a Beverly Hills park. He pleaded no contest and paid a fine and the incident led to the singer coming out of the closet. Far from being ruined by the scandal, Michael later even defended his decision to cruise public toilets for gay sex!
