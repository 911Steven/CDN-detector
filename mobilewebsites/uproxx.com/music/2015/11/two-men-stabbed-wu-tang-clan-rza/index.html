Two Men Reportedly Stabbed In The Home Of Wu-Tang Clan Member RZA

By: Andrew Roberts 11.08.15

Getty Image / Michael Loccisano

Two men are reportedly in serious condition after a stabbing incident at the home of Wu-Tang Clan founder RZA. The rapper, whose real name is Robert Diggs, owns the house in New Jersey, but it was unclear if he was home at the time of the incident according to NJ.com:

New Jersey State Police responded to a report of a stabbing at a Deer Trail Drive home at 1:18 a.m. on Saturday, said Sgt. Gregory Williams, a State Police spokesman.
He said the two men sustained serious injuries and were taken to Jersey Shore University Medical Center in Neptune. The hospital did not have a patient named Robert Diggs there on Saturday.

Police are investigating a double stabbing at RZA's New Jersey home https://t.co/0IeJ9uXgfQ pic.twitter.com/VbY6xQPiOo

— SPIN (@SPINmagazine) November 8, 2015

Police would not confirm that the home was indeed owned by the Wu-Tang clan star, but the records for the address indicate that Diggs is the owner according to NJ.com. This comes both on the heels of the news that the FBI was investigating the group and that the RZA would be donating to the Hip Hop Chess Foundation according to XXL Magazine:

The HHCF will be teaching the mental strategy game to incarcerated and at risk youth in St. Louis thanks in large part to a considerable donation made by Wu-Tang Clan general. RZA also serves as a board member for the non-profit which helps spread love for the game of chess.

We’ll have any updates as they are released.

(Via NJ.Com/XXL Mag)
