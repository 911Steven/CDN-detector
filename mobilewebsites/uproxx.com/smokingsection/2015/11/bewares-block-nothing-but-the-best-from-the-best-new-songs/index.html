BEWARE’s Block: Nothing But The Best, From The Best

By: Beware 11.08.15

Red Pill – “’90s Money”

If you’re old enough to remember the first time Timbs were cool, you’ll remember a time when rappers made way more cash off their craft. Throughout his new record “90s Money,” Red Pill remembers those prosperous times over rockin’ production from Alex Goose, cleverly name dropping all sorts of applicable topics from the era of Doug Funnie and flannels, in an effort to up his paper stacks. Help the Detroit rapper’s cause, by pre-ordering his upcoming Day Drunk EP here.

Caleborate Feat. G-Eazy – “We Want It All”

A few months back, Caleborate was featured in this column, after I was heavily digging his Hella Good project. Now, he’s got a song with G-Eazy called “We Want It All,” which has almost 400k plays on Soundcloud. This soulful jam finds the Cali MC working towards greater heights every day, which he appears to be well on his way to achieving. Download via iTunes.

Emoney – “Sorry”

Sometimes, all you can do is apologize, to safe face. On “Sorry,” R&B rapper Emoney spills his sorrows over super-cool radio-ready production from Sergio, vowing to do whatever it takes to earn back his better half. The Cleveland artist’s fluid flow is impressive, so keep an eye out for his upcoming Studio Luv album, dropping in Feb.

Subscribe to UPROXX

Paul Akujor – “Superhero”

Original concepts in R&B are rare, bur Paul Akujor has pulled it off on “Superhero.” The slow-moving groove calls out to all our favorite comic book superstars, using magnetic production to help justify the somewhat nerdy subject matter. Sounds good to me.

Gemaine – “Gotta Have You”

Another strong R&B talent coming out of the woodwork is Gemaine, who recently released his Curious EP to much fanfare. The ten track project from the Compton prodigy features more than a few instances of greatness, but my favorite was”Gotta Have You,” a bouncy offering that showcases his ability to hit the notes and encapsulate multiple genres at once.

MME – “Penny Bank (Peace God)”

St Louis collective MME must be deep dudes, because there are a lot of levels of their new single “Penny Bank.” The heavy record starts off soft and soulful, then evolves into a more frustrated tone, eventually coming back full circle to the original beat. The track is quite creative and show’s the group’s range.

Larry g(EE) – “Walk Away (Demo Version)”

Not quite an R&B or pop record, Larry g(EE)’s “Walk Away” demo feels like a vintage vinyl from the 50s or 60s, offering a swinging groove that reminds listeners to steer clear of questionable relationships. Aside from the drum kit, everything about this jam feels retro, and it’s quite refreshing. Bop along, daddy-o.

Smuv & Miraa – “Sergio (Out Now)”

At a mere minute and twenty-five seconds, Smuv & Miraa manage to evoke quite a bit of comfort with their new instrumental single “Sergio.” The small slice of audible happiness is from their upcoming Under Palm Trees tape (buy here) and is the type of song you put on repeat during the cocktail hour of a Hip-Hop friendly wedding. Cheers!
